Frequently asked questions

How does this differ from PDFWords?

PDFWords extracts every word from the PDFs automatically (an open, exploratory approach). PDFTerms does the opposite: you control the vocabulary, listing exactly the terms of interest. This is the directed approach, well suited to systematic reviews and to testing predefined concepts.

How do I group synonyms?

Use the equals sign. Example: CO2 = carbon dioxide; dióxido de carbono. All the variants are counted as a single term, with the label to the left of "=". Separate the variants with a semicolon or comma. You can also let the app generate the synonyms automatically from the library.

How do I use wildcards and compound terms?

The * captures variations: emission* matches emission, emissions, emitting. Compound terms work directly: shore power searches for the whole phrase. The search ignores case and accents.

What is binary counting per document?

In the "Document" scope, two terms co-occur if they appear in the same PDF, counted once per document. This prevents a long PDF that repeats a term many times from dominating the network. It is the recommended bibliometric standard. In the "Sentence" scope, co-occurrence is counted for every sentence in which the two terms appear together.

How do categories appear in VOSviewer?

Each category in [ ] becomes a cluster in the JSON, and VOSviewer assigns a distinct colour to each cluster automatically. Terms without a category fall into a neutral group.

Some terms were not found — what now?

The result lists the terms with zero occurrences, for quality control. Check the spelling and accents, try a wildcard (e.g. govern*) or add synonyms. Scanned PDFs (image only) require OCR first.
